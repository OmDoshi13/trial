# Trenkwalder Group - Company Policy Handbook

## 1. Code of Conduct

All employees are expected to act with integrity, respect, and professionalism. Trenkwalder is committed to fostering an inclusive and diverse workplace where every individual is valued.

### 1.1 Anti-Discrimination Policy
Trenkwalder does not tolerate discrimination based on race, gender, age, religion, sexual orientation, disability, or national origin. Any incidents of discrimination should be reported to HR immediately.

### 1.2 Conflict of Interest
Employees must disclose any potential conflicts of interest to their manager and HR. This includes secondary employment, financial interests in competitors, or personal relationships that may influence business decisions.

## 2. Data Protection & Privacy

### 2.1 GDPR Compliance
As a European company, Trenkwalder strictly complies with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). All employees must:
- Handle personal data with care and only for legitimate business purposes
- Report any data breaches to the Data Protection Officer within 24 hours
- Complete annual data protection training
- Not transfer personal data outside the EU without proper safeguards

### 2.2 Confidentiality
Confidential business information must not be shared with unauthorized persons. This includes client data, financial information, trade secrets, and internal strategies. Confidentiality obligations continue after employment ends.

## 3. Information Security

### 3.1 Password Policy
- Passwords must be at least 12 characters long
- Must contain uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters
- Must be changed every 90 days
- Never share passwords or write them down
- Use the company password manager (1Password)

### 3.2 Device Security
- All devices must have full-disk encryption enabled
- Screen lock must activate after 5 minutes of inactivity
- Lost or stolen devices must be reported to IT immediately
- Software must only be installed from approved sources

## 4. Expense Policy

### 4.1 Travel Expenses
- Domestic travel: Economy class for flights under 4 hours, business class for longer flights
- International travel: Business class for flights over 4 hours
- Hotel: Up to €150 per night in standard cities, up to €200 in high-cost cities (London, Paris, Munich)
- Daily meal allowance: €50 per day for domestic travel, €70 for international travel

### 4.2 Expense Reporting
- All expenses must be submitted within 30 days of being incurred
- Receipts are required for all expenses over €25
- Submit expense reports through the HR portal
- Manager approval required for all claims
- Reimbursement is processed within 10 business days

## 5. Performance Management

### 5.1 Annual Reviews
- Performance reviews are conducted annually in Q4
- Mid-year check-ins occur in Q2
- Both employee self-assessment and manager assessment are required
- Performance ratings: Exceptional, Exceeds Expectations, Meets Expectations, Needs Improvement

### 5.2 Goals & OKRs
- Each employee sets 3-5 annual goals aligned with team and company objectives
- Goals are reviewed quarterly
- OKR framework is used for team-level objectives

## 6. Compensation & Benefits

### 6.1 Salary Reviews
- Annual salary reviews in January
- Based on performance, market data, and company results
- Adjustments effective from April 1st

### 6.2 Bonus Structure
- Performance bonus: Up to 10% of annual salary based on individual performance
- Team bonus: Up to 5% based on team objectives
- Company bonus: Up to 5% based on company financial results
